Snuffbox associated with Flora MacDonald


(Image from https://www.nms.ac.uk/explore-our-collections/collection-search-results/snuffbox/16512)

Flora MacDonald famously helped Bonnie Prince Charlie escape from Scotland following the defeat of the Jacobite army at Culloden on 16 April 1746. This tortoiseshell snuffbox, a treasured heirloom of her clan, links the romantic pair. It has a portrait of James VIII and III on the lid and bears the inscription A gift from Prince Charles Stewart to Miss Flora Macdonald 1746.

Who was Flora Macdonald?

Flora MacDonald was born in the Outer Hebrides, in 1722. After her fathers death, she was brought up under the care of the chief of her clan, the Macdonalds of Clanranald.

In 1746, 24-year-old Flora was living on the neighbouring island of Benbecula. And it was here that she met Bonnie Prince Charlie and stepped into the pages of history.

Flora MacDonald and Bonnie Prince Charlie

After his defeat, Prince Charles Edward had been led to safety by a Jacobite general. But, as a wanted man with a price on his head, escaping Scotland would not be easy.

By late April he and four companions had made their way through a storm to Benbecula. Here they met Flora, who assisted Charles escape and smuggled him to the Isle of Skye dressed, at her suggestion, as her serving maid Betty Burke.

What happened to Flora?

Flora was arrested and spent a year in prison. She returned to Skye, where she married Allan MacDonald of Kingsburgh.

The following year, the Flora and her husband emigrated to America but her husband was captured and imprisoned after fighting for the British in the War of Independence, so in 1779, Flora returned to Skye. Flora died in 1790, at the age of 68, and she was buried in Kilmuir cemetery, in Skye.

What happened to Bonnie Prince Charlie?

Charles escaped Scotland forever on a French frigate in 1746. It was the end of the Stuart attempt to reclaim the British throne. He died from a stroke on 31 January 1788, also aged 68.
